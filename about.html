About Me.

I kicked around the idea of becoming a programmer/developer for a while, but I never really got serious with it until I was in the Air Force. My first class was an intro to programming, which was C++. My next language I started learning was Python, which would coincidently become my favorite language. I started playing around with Python's web framework Django and really took to web development. I started doing The Odin Project, which led me deeper into JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and eventually Ruby on Rails, which I've really taken a liking to. My friends who are developers have all told me the same thing--that JavaScript is the future. Which leads me to where I am today.

I'm really hoping that what I get from VWC is a little more of a structured curriculum than what I'm used to. The catch-22 about having unlimited resources is that it's hardly possible to find a consitant course, let alone one that doesn't cost tens of thousands of dollars. I don't only want to learn about web development, I want to learn the right way. Truthfully, my absolute dream is to in some and some way be a developer and I certainly believe that I have the passion and drive.
